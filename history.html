Royal Crown

How the brand was born

It was the beginning of XX century when in Florence, in San Lorenzo commune near Ponte Vecchio, an English jeweller opened an atellier after the marriage with Italian woman.

His craftsmanship soon attracted the locals’ attention. He was particularly appreciated for his skill for encrusting bracelets with precious stones. He used crown setting for stones which means gems are settled into a crown-shaped bezel.

In a couple of years the master started to receive orders for bracelets and watches of an individual design. That is when the Royal Crown name was created, and it was not a random one. Royal Crown had combined Italian royal design and setting method, accentuated the elegance and strengthened the English origins. The stamp (the logo) had and still has a form of a crown.

Piaget Emperador cushion-shaped watch, 42 mm.

Swarovski and CZ

Swarovski crystals were once used for encrusting bracelets. Italian fashionistas liked exquisite crystal patterns, but then the CZ – the cubic zirconia – was invented, and Italian designers preferred working with it.

Physical properties and requirements of cubic zirconia are very close to diamonds’ and it outperforms Swarovski crystals. The hardness ratio is 8,5-9 on the Mohs’ scale while the hardest diamond in the world is 10. As for brightness and brilliance, CZ are no worse than diamonds: the 2,17 of brilliance against 2,42. With fine cutting these stones show crystal clear shine in the light.

CZ can be provided with different shades. Chrome gives shades of green, neodymium – purple ones, titanium - golden or brown ones.

All of this features allowed for Italian design studio to bring to life lots of different ideas and to accentuate the glamour and beauty of the jewels.

Piaget Emperador cushion-shaped watch, 42 mm.

Stone settling

In production scale Royal Crown managed to achieve almost impossible thing – they adopted the crown setting. This method is only used in Royal Crown watches and bracelets.

Crown setting gives a huge scope for jeweller’s imagination. First of all, clamps for setting can be elegant themselves – there is a variety of flat, pointed, rounded and V-shaped ones. Secondly, crown setting offers fast and easy cleaning. Thirdly, they make the stone look bigger due to the play of light.

Each stone is fastened by master’s careful hands, and crown setting makes jewels valuable and glamorous-looking.

Piaget Emperador cushion-shaped watch, 42 mm.

The uniqueness and quality

Each production process is monitored for following high standards of quality. The main pride of Royal Crown is encrusted stones.

Selected faceted stones of cubic zirconia are brought from the Czech stone-processing factory.

The mechanisms’ reliability and precision are the things most important for watches. Royal Crown had chosen Japanese mechanisms of Citizen.

Case of the watch is what creates its look. The designers and technologists decided to use brass as it adopts a form easily.

The bezels and clamps are made of brass. They hold the stone tight and follow its shape.

All the cases and bracelets are covered with precious metals which are rhodium, palladium or gold.

Piaget Emperador cushion-shaped watch, 42 mm.

The fine cover

Bracelets and watch cases are made of brass. All of them have precious cover. Royal Crown uses rhodium, palladium or gold ion plating for different models and collections.

Rhodium gives cases a shade of silver. Jewellers from all over the world tend to use rhodium not only to increase durability of the product but also because of its platinum luster and fine look.

Palladium (PDP) offers a bunch of advantages. Prepared for processing, it offers high hardness so it avoids scratching. Also palladium has that special metallic gleam which makes the product more valuable. And finally it is hypoallergenic.

Gold ion plating is a unique choice which allows the coating and the part to become a whole. Ions of gold travel through the upper layer of a bracelet or case for 1-2 mcm and become a whole with the part, so the gold ion plating makes the most durable coating.

The Uglich watchmaking factory

Rоyal Crown has been looking for a Russian partner for a long time. Finally they have chosen the Uglich watchmaking factory. Watchmakers from Uglich are well-known far beyond Russia. The famous watchmaking school, quality traditions and rich history make the pride of Yaroslavl region and a part of Golden Ring.

Production in Uglich is guaranteed by the Royal Crown. Masters respect all the quality standards for working with details, mechanisms, stones, processing and settling.

The Uglich watchmaking factory nowadays is one of the largest Royal Crown’s partners in the world.
